First, the most important thing is to organize the key knowledge points.
We need to understand that all exam questions are built around the most basic concepts and knowledge points.
The purpose of doing exercises is to strengthen and master those knowledge points.
So, spend ten minutes every day closing your book and drawing a mind map of the chapter from memory.
For each knowledge point, connect it in your mind with a typical example question, or with a question you once answered incorrectly.
In this way, you can link knowledge points with actual problems.
At the same time, compare your mind map with the textbook to check what is missing, and turn scattered knowledge into a complete system.
Even if the question types change in the exam, you will still be able to quickly identify the tested knowledge point.
You will not get completely stuck just because the question looks unfamiliar, and you will be able to secure the basic marks.
Second, practise calculation.
In exams, many unnecessary marks are lost because of calculation mistakes.
When practising, we should make our rough work and reasoning process clear and organized, so that we can trace back and check our work.
Every time we make a mistake, we should record the reason and do targeted practice repeatedly, turning calculation into a stable habit.
In addition, calculation mistakes often happen because we feel rushed.
At that moment, we must slow down and keep a steady pace.
Many students also make calculation errors because they do not clearly understand the principles, reasoning, and methods behind the calculations.
In that case, we need to strengthen the knowledge points related to calculation.
Third, practise by focusing on weak areas in layers.
Do not blindly do full sets of exam papers.
Instead, find your weaknesses, such as problem-solving methods you are not familiar with, or types of questions you do not know how to handle well.
After identifying your weak areas, practise in groups of three to five questions, moving step by step from easier to harder problems.
For questions you repeatedly get wrong, review them again after a few days.
By actively fixing your own weak spots, even if the exam is more difficult than expected, you can still steadily earn marks on basic and medium-level questions.
When you meet difficult questions, their ideas are often developed step by step from medium-level questions.
Once you have firmly mastered the thinking behind medium-level questions, you will also be able to solve at least the first part of harder questions.
We emphasize systematic deliberate practice.
The essence of this kind of practice is not to guarantee a high score in every exam, but to turn aimless hard work into a system that supports stable performance.
Math exam questions will always contain uncertainties.
However, a solid knowledge framework and stable problem-solving habits can help us reduce most exam risks.
